JUNE 2014 - CURRENT

Winter Study Research Project
Williams College Computer Science Department
Williamstown, MA
Advisor: Duane Bailey, William Lenhart

Given a finite nonempty sequence S of integers, write it as XYk, where Yk is a power of greatest exponent that is a suffix of S: this k is the curling number of S. The curling number conjecture is that if one starts with any initial sequence S, and extends it by repeatedly appending the curling number of the current sequence, the sequence will eventually reach 1. The paper "On Curling Numbers of Integer Sequences" describes curling number in detail. The project focused on just the sequences of 2's and 3's.

  • Designed algorithms to analyze the pattern in the curling sequence expansion and deduced that sequences with starting length greater than 10 eventually have a converging tail.
  • Contributed to the sequence database by generating curling expansion of sequences with initial length greater than 48, which is not covered in the paper. The generation is still underway and uses GPU and FPGAs rather than CPU.
  • Deduced that complete expansion of sequences of shorter lengths are most of the time completely contained in the expansion of longer sequences.
  • Currently also analyzing rotten sequences, sequences whose expansion length decreases by appending 2 or 3 at the end of the sequence.
Programming Languages Used: C, Python
